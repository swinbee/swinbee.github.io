Swinburne Integration Bee

An integration bee is a mathematical competition focussed on integral calculus. After a qualifying round, competitors challenge each other at being the fastest and most accurate integrator, much in the likes of spelling bees. One of the most celebrated and popular integration bees in the world is that of Massachussets Institute of Technology. It was held for the first time in 1981.

SwInBee is run and managed by the Department of Mathematics and is open to all undergraduate students at Swinburne.

When and where

The first SwInBee will be held on Friday, 14th September, 2018, from 10 am till 12 pm, in room EN. Please be in the room by 9:50 am.

Rules and Schedule

The qualifier round lasts 30 minutes and consists of 30 questions of various difficulties, where only the final answer is marked. Workings do not attract any mark. NO books or calculators are allowed.

After the qualifier round, there will be a break for 25 minutes, with pizza and drinks. The best 4 participants in the qualifier round will then go "head-to-head" on the whiteboard to decide who is the Queen or King Integrator!
